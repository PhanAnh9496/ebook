The Unified Modeling Language, second edition by Booch, Rumbaugh and Jacobson is certainly the most accurate reference and is also well written. But it's 450 pages thick !
My preference is Martin Fowler's UML distilled, third edition. It's not a full coverage of UML, but focused on a practical subset. It's even not orthodox on some points. But it's damn effective and well written. As a remarkable feature of this book, through three editions, it never grows over 150 pages !
